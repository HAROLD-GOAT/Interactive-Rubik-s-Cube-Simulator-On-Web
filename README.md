so, I didn't put and lot of time into giving appropriate variable names and I didn't add any comments (except in 
a few lines).  My way of doing it might not be the best possible method and it is also not optimized.  But I am sure
that this is one of the best rubik's cube simulator out there.

Note: This is just an interactive 3D rubik's cube
* The faces can be moved by left clicking and dragging
* The faces will follow the user's mouse when left clicked and dragged
* The face will automatically snap to an appropriate position when the mouse button is released
* Rotate the entire cube by clicked and dragging outside the cube
* If you want to get it to its original state you can just refresh the window.
